About UX

  • Understanding your users (from your users)
    users only use things when the product is value-added to their life
  • Identify your users first (target audience)
  • Empathy for your users is key (Pre-design)
  • Be predictable for your users
  • Behavior patterns

History of UX

  • 4000BC: Feng Shui (風水)
  • 500BC: Ergonomics (人體工學)
  • The 1900s: Taylorism / Scientific Management (科學管理) / Dehumanizing
  • The 1940s: Toyota / value feedbacks for better process
  • 1955: Henry Dreyfus’s “Design for PEOPLE”
  • The 1970~80s: Xerox / Apple / PC / GUIs
  • The 1990s: Donald Norman
  • The 2000s: Accessibility & Inclusivity (WCAG)

Usabilities Goals of UX/UI

What do you want your users to do/think after their experience?

  1. “I got what I need.”
  2. “That wasn’t so hard.”
    Prioritize your users and your time and efforts.
  3. “I really enjoyed that.”
    Make personas specific.
  4. “It’s become a habit.”
  5. “I’ll tell my friends about this.”
    Building royalty.

Usability Goals

“Ubiquitous Computing”?

  • Privacy Violation
  • Internet of Things
  • Availability
  • Nice but Creepy
  • Data-driven
  • Immersion

Anytime, anything, anywhere, with network

  • WHAT: the growing trend of embedding computational capability - generally in the form of microprocessors
  • WHERE: into everyday objects
  • WHY: To make them effectively communicate and perform useful tasks
  • HOW: in a way that minimizes the end user’s need to interact with computers as computers

Ubicomp relies on…

  • Contextual data aggregation and application
  • Seamless, intuitive access points
  • Flexible payment systems
Exciting vs. Invisible: Turning products into the experience

Key Features

  • The Human is on Centerstage
  • Inexpensive but Many Processors
  • Processing Real-Time Data
  • Constant Connectivity and Availability
  • Greater Surveillance

Components of UX

  • Utility: How useful is it? / subjective
  • Functional Integrity: How does it operate?
  • Usability: How easy is it to use? / learning curve
  • Persuasiveness (說服力): How much it can be trusted?
  • Graphic Design: How does it look like? / first impression

Design Thinking

Empathize → Define (Problem) → Ideate (Brainstorm for solution) → Prototype (Easily changing version) → Test (loop)

Design is a never-ending process.

Quantity before quality during Ideate.

Roles of UX

  • UX Designer: the one who covers all 5 steps of design thinking
  • Product Designer: overlaps with UX Designer but focus on business perspective more (product roadmap)
  • Visual Designer: focus on creating Prototype step
  • UX Researcher: takes the part at the beginning from Empathize to Ideate and also the Test step Focus Group Discussion(FGD) 焦點團體訪談
  • Content Strategist: focus on the content(text) of the Prototype step
