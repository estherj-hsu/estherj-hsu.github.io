UX Research

  • To understand users’ needs, behaviors, experiences, and motivations
  • It’s done through various qualitative and quantitative methods

The process of understanding the impact of design on an audience.

  • Until you know your user and their needs, emotions, feelings, struggles, etc., you won’t be able to deliver a great user experience.

Stages of the product life cycle

  1. Discovery
    • Questions
      • What do users need?
      • What is already working and what isn’t?
    • Methods: 1-on-1 user interviews, focus group, competitive analysis, benchmark studies, ethnographic research
  2. Development
    • Questions
      • Do users understand how it works?
      • How do users interact with your prototype?
      • Can users find what they are looking for?
      • Do the features look and feel right?
    • Methods:
      moderated and unmoderated usability testing(有指引/ 無指引易用性測試), prototype testing, card sorting(卡片分類法), preference testing(偏好測試), A/B testing
  3. Live
    • Goal: Measuring how well the product meets users’ needs
    • Key: Proactively adapt and iterate if needed
    • Methods: surveys, data analytics, bug reports

Quantity VS Quality

  • Quantity: Observes what can be measured numerically
  • Quality: Observes why particular trends or patterns occur

Attitudes VS Behaviors

  • Attitudes: Seeks to assess why users have particular attitudes and feelings towards an experience
  • Behaviors: focuses on what users do with the product in question

Data-driven & Model-driven

Data-driven Inquiry

  • Contextual Inquiry (脈絡訪查)
    Takes time.
    • Understanding in context: direct observation and discussions or actual setting in which it takes place
    • Partnership with subjects: engaged with participants in a joint effort to understand and make sense of their work practice
    • Focused investigation
    • Five elaborate models
      • Flow Model
      • Sequence Model
      • Cultural Model
      • Artifact Model
      • Physical Model

Model-driven Inquiry

Build models as the basis for limited, sharply focused data gathering.

Case studies and personas are popular models.

  1. Build exploratory models
  2. Compile questions and issues
  3. Select expeditious means for resolution
  4. Conduct limited, highly focused inquiry
  5. Refine and complete the initial models
  6. Review and validate the models

Purpose

  • To create preliminary models for later refinement and use
  • To uncover areas

Benchmarking (基準化分析) in UX

Methods for benchmarking in UX

Using metrics to process data

  • Retrospective Benchmarking Remember that human memory is fallible
  • Task-based Benchmarking Moderated testing (適性測驗)

Steps

  1. Choose what to measure
    • Tasks: Figure out tasks and break them down into a list, then prioritize them by importance
    • Metrics: HEART
      • *H*appiness
      • *E*ngagement
      • *A*doption
      • *R*etention: how often
      • *T*ask effectiveness and efficiency
  2. Decide how to measure
    • Usability Testing
    • Survey: behavior, background, or opinion tools: optimal workshop / hotjar
    • Analytics
  3. First measurement
    • Measuring Competitor Scores
    • Using Industry Benchmarks: NPS(Net promoter Score)
    • Comparing against the stakeholder-determined goal
  4. (RE)Design your project
  5. Collect additional measurements
  6. Interpret findings
  7. Repeat Steps 4-7
