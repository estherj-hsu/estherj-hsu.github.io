About AFDS

Aboriginal Front Door Society(AFDS) is a culturally safe, peer-designed non-judgemental place for Aboriginal Peoples their friends, and family in the Downtown East Side. They create hope and offer support through Aboriginal cultural traditions, caring, and acceptance.

Esther

I heard a lot about First Nation before coming to Vancouver, and after I got here, I heard a lot about the people in downtown east side. So when I knew that I could choose either an NGO or a local business website to redesign for our final project, I decided to go for Aboriginal Front Door Society (AFDS).

Problem Statement & Research

Problem

The website of an NGO should make people understand more about the organization and help them to make the decision on donation. And according to the current AFDS website, it seems that it didn't reach the goal of an NGO website.

HMW

HMW improve the AFDS website so that people can learn more about the organization and even help them to make a donation based on the information they get on the website.

Users Interviews

Findings

  • Authenticity is essential
  • Multiple payment methods are important and also increase the trustful
  • It’s not easy to find out what AFDS is doing from the website
  • Overall content is too wordy and it’s hard to find the important information at the first glance
  • The topics/field of the NGO matter to the users!
  • Show the transparency of AFDS, like words from the people they help

Usability Testing

Low Fidelity Testing

Since it's an NGO website, I chose to focus on the Donation Flow for the usability tests.

After interviewing 3 participants, I got the most important finding -- Users want to know more about how their donation could help.

Solution

Adding more information about how donations will help on the donation page

High Fidelity Testing

Testing Flows

  • Making a finacial donation on AFDS
  • Finding the information to access the AFDS Food Bank

Final Design

Conclusion

What did I learn?

The AFDS website is a content-heavy one, and I understand that as an NGO, they have lots of stories want to tell. But I think it's also essential to consider the users. Telling a story is a great way to brand, but how to tell a concise story is even more important.

What could I improve or do more?

If I had enough time to work on this project, I think I would like to go to AFDS in person and know them more. Also, digging a bit deeper into the donation platform in Canada to see how it works. Maybe I would come up with other solutions to improve the donation process.
